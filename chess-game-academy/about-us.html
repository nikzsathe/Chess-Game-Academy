About Us

Chess Game Academy high-quality instruction and professionally organized competitions for young chess players of all skill levels. We train beginners to gain a thorough understanding of the game, beginning with the basics. Aside from that, we offer adequate training to help beginners gradually learn to play the game with skill and tact.
Chess Game Academy is dedicated to providing young students with effective chess instruction. The game of chess is known to help children develop valuable critical thinking and creative problem-solving skills that can later be applied in math and science. Chess is a universal learning tool that provides a unique combination of learning and playing.

Mission

The mission of our academy is to identify upcoming players and develop them into world-class professionals.
To achieve this lofty goal, we provide them with professional coaching before sending them to tournaments.

History

The academy, which was started by chess coach Mr. Gauraav Vengurlekar, provides a variety of educational opportunities for students. With the right curriculum of education, any learner can become a competitive chess player. That is precisely what Mr. Gauraav Vengurlekar's all-inclusive course accomplishes, providing every learner with the precise content they want at every developmental level.Our Chess Academy is committed to giving young kids top-notch chess instruction. Chess is an all-purpose learning tool that provides a unique blend of learning and pleasure. It is well recognized to foster in kids the critical thinking and creative problem-solving abilities that are later utilized in math and science.
We offer active learning strategies including putting effort into solving puzzles, memorization for better memory, and regular testing and evaluation to guarantee successful learning. It's a sport, chess! Kids who want to participate in chess competitions are given the greatest instruction possible on how to get better and get ready for competition. A crucial component of learning to play chess is competing. Along with developing many excellent skills, it also teaches people how to handle pressure, accept defeat gracefully, and respect both friends and rivals.
